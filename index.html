Months

The months are pretty straightforward. You just say the number of the month followed by ‘月’, pronounced gatsu, and you’re done. No extra symbols or variations. So for example ‘1月’ is January, ‘2月’ is February, and so on.

Days

In Japanese the days of the week are named for the Chinese philosphies of yin and yang and the five elements of Taoism. The Japanese adopted a Gregorian calendar system in the early 20th century but they already had a seven-day system.

  • 月げつMoon (Yin)
  • 火かFire
  • 水すいWater
  • 木もくWood
  • 金きんGold
  • 土どEarth
  • 日にちSun (Yang)

Dates

In English, you’re used to seeing ‘th’ when talking about the date. The 4th, the 5th, the 6th, etc. In Japanese however, you’ll be seeing ‘日’, pronounced ‘nichi’ when talking about the date. 4日, 5日, 6日, etc. But just like in English, the endings aren’t all the same, despite their appearance. These irregular endings are shown below.

  • １日ついたち
  • ２日ふつか
  • ３日みっか
  • ４日よっか
  • ５日いつか
  • ６日むいか
  • ７日なのか
  • ８日ようか
  • ９日ここのか
  • １０日とうか
  • １４日じゅうよっか
  • ２０日はつか
  • ２４日にじゅうよっか
